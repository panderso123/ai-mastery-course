## Thread 2 — The prompt-length law

```
Most people make their AI prompts longer when the output gets worse.

That's exactly backwards.

—

1/ Long prompts produce SHORTER, sharper output.
Short prompts produce LONGER, vaguer output.

Sounds wrong. Then you watch it happen.

—

2/ A long, structured prompt constrains the model.

Role. Phases. Constraints. Format.

Nowhere to wander.

—

3/ A short prompt is a hand-wave.

The model has to guess at all of those.

It guesses by producing more text — covering its bases.

—

4/ Here's the structure of a real production prompt:

PHASE 1 — silently build a voice profile
PHASE 2 — generate 4 drafts using the profile
PHASE 3 — return in this exact format
End: a one-paragraph self-audit

Every word does work.

—

5/ Try the structure on your own writing today.

Compare.
```

## Tips for posting these

- **Don't post all three the same week.** Space them 3–5 days apart.
- **Edit the names and numbers.** "Aman" and "$4,200" are placeholders. Use real students if you have them; if you don't, remove the social proof section entirely. Fabricated case studies kill trust faster than no case studies.
- **Pin the strongest one** to your profile during launch week.
- **Quote-tweet replies** with mini-explanations when people push back. Public objections handled gracefully sell better than the original thread.
