# M08 — Short-form: Reels, Shorts, TikTok

> **Module 8 of 20** · Part 2: Content Workflows
> **Estimated time:** 45 minutes (plus filming)
> **Outcome:** 9 distinct short-form scripts, 3 of them filmed in one sitting.

## Premise

Short-form lives or dies in the **first three seconds.** A viewer who isn't hooked at 0:03 is gone before 0:04. So short-form prompts must be obsessed with the opener, with pattern interrupts, and with on-screen text that completes (or contradicts) the audio.

## Concept — The 3-second hook + the 7-second pattern interrupt

| Time | Job to be done | Common moves |
|------|----------------|--------------|
| 0:00–0:03 | **Stop the scroll** | Bold visual change, contrarian claim, on-screen text that completes the audio. |
| 0:03–0:07 | **Buy the next 7 seconds** | Pattern interrupt: cut, zoom, prop, second on-screen line. |
| 0:07–0:25 | **Deliver the goods** | One specific point. One specific number. One specific name. *Not three.* |
| 0:25–0:40 | **Payoff + setup the loop** | Punchline that resolves the hook and seeds curiosity to rewatch. |
| 0:40+ | **CTA / loop** | Either explicit CTA (follow for X) or visual return to the opening frame. |

## Walkthrough — Topic to scripts

**Topic:** "The email subject line that books 3x more sales calls."

The short-form prompt produces **3 distinct scripts.** Format:

```text
SHORT 1 — angle: counter-conventional
[0:00] HOOK    "Most sales emails open with 'Quick question.'"  | TEXT: "And get ignored."
[0:03] BEAT 1  "I tested 11 subject lines on 4,200 cold emails." | CUT: to spreadsheet
[0:10] BEAT 2  "The winner was 4 words long..."                 | TEXT: "4 words."
[0:25] PAYOFF  "'Quick favor about [their company].'"            | TEXT: full subject line
[0:40] CTA     "Try it tomorrow. Tell me your reply rate."       | CUT: back to opening frame
```

Notice the **TEXT overlays** — they don't repeat the audio; they *complete* it or *contradict* it. That's what makes thumbs stop.

## Exercise — Batch produce 9 shorts in 90 minutes

1. Pick **3 topics** you actually care about.
2. Run the short-form prompt for each (Practice Kit `03_Prompts/03_shortform.md`). 9 scripts total.
3. **Film 3 in one sitting.** Same outfit, same lighting. Don't re-shoot for perfect.
4. Edit using the on-screen text overlays from the script.
5. Schedule across 9 days.

**Acceptance test:** the 3 you film all hit the 0:03 hook test. Show them to one other person; if they don't watch past 5 seconds, the hook is wrong.

> ⚠️ **Pitfall.** Don't write 9 scripts and film *one*. The momentum of batch filming is the entire reason this workflow works. Half-finished batches become permanent half-finished batches.

## Knowledge check

1. What is the on-screen text overlay's job, exactly?
2. Why do shorts deliver one specific point, not three?
3. What's the practical reason to film 3 in one sitting?

### Answer key

1. Complete or contradict the audio — never duplicate it. Overlays that just transcribe the audio waste pixels and bore the viewer.
2. Cognitive load. A 30-second video can land one specific claim memorably. Three claims dilute each other and the viewer remembers none.
3. Cost-of-context-switching. Setup, lighting, look, energy — the marginal cost of the second and third short, after the first, is near zero. The first one cost everything.

## Takeaway

Shorts compound when batched. Don't shoot one. Shoot three, schedule nine, repeat. The hook is the only thing that matters in week one of any new short-form account.
