# M10 — Building your content rhythm

> **Module 10 of 20** · Part 2: Content Workflows
> **Estimated time:** 30 minutes
> **Outcome:** A weekly cadence on your calendar that you'll actually keep.

## Premise

Modules 6–9 give you the generation engine. This one gives you the schedule that runs the engine without burning you out. Most content systems fail not because the loops are bad but because the rhythm is unsustainable.

## Concept — The default weekly cadence

| Day | Time block | What happens |
|-----|------------|--------------|
| **Monday** | 60 min | **Idea capture.** Note 7 tweet ideas, 1 long-form idea, 3 short-form angles. Don't write — just list. |
| **Tuesday** | 90 min | **Tweet batch.** Run M6's loop on Monday's 7 ideas. Edit, schedule for Wed–Tue. |
| **Wednesday** | 120 min | **Long-form recording.** Run M7's prompt → script → record. Single take if possible. |
| **Thursday** | 60 min | **Shorts batch.** Run M9 to extract 3 shorts from Wed's transcript. Film all 3 in 30 min. |
| **Friday** | 30 min | **Repurpose & schedule.** Newsletter from M9's recipe. Schedule everything across next 9 days. |
| **Sat / Sun** | — | **Off.** Engagement and replies happen organically; no production. |

Total: ~6 hours / week. Output: ~1 long-form, ~3 shorts, ~7 tweets, ~1 newsletter section per week.

## Walkthrough — Tuning the cadence to your reality

**You only have 3 hours/week.** Drop the long-form. Run Monday + Tuesday + Thursday. You'll ship ~7 tweets and ~3 shorts/week, no long-form. Still a win.

**You only have 1 hour/week.** Run Monday + a 60-min Tuesday block on tweets only. Ship 5 tweets/week. *That alone* will outperform 80% of operators in your niche, indefinitely.

**You have 10+ hours/week.** Add a second long-form on Friday and double the shorts batch. Same loops, doubled inputs.

> ✅ **Win pattern.** Pick a cadence you can keep on your *worst* week, not your best. Bad weeks happen. The system that survives them compounds; the system that requires perfect weeks dies.

## Concept — The metrics that matter (and don't)

Track:

- **Per-output engagement** (replies, saves, DMs) — the strongest signal.
- **Inbound conversations** (DMs, emails, calls booked) — the signal that pays bills.
- **Weeks shipped without missing** — the meta-signal. The number that matters most over a year.

Don't track:

- Daily follower count. Mood-killer; not actionable.
- Like counts. Likes are hot air; replies and DMs are signal.
- Per-tweet view counts in week 1. Long-tail dwarfs the first 24 hours on tweets that work.

## Exercise — Set your rhythm

1. Open your calendar.
2. Block the cadence above (or your reduced version) for the next **8 weeks**.
3. Treat the blocks like external meetings. *No reschedules into themselves.*
4. At the end of week 4 and week 8, run a 15-min retrospective: which loops to keep, which to drop.

**Acceptance test:** you've shipped at least one output every week for 8 weeks consecutive. Skip a week and the system breaks; the muscle is "consistency under noise."

## Knowledge check

1. What's the default weekly time investment for the full cadence?
2. Which metric matters most over a one-year horizon?
3. What's the first cadence change to make if you only have 3 hours/week?

### Answer key

1. ~6 hours per week.
2. Weeks shipped without missing. Compound interest beats peak performance.
3. Drop long-form. Keep tweet generation and shorts batches. The two highest-leverage loops survive a 50% time cut.

## Takeaway

Rhythm beats intensity. A 6-hour week run for 50 weeks crushes a 30-hour week run for 8. Ship every week. Tune the cadence; never skip the cadence.
