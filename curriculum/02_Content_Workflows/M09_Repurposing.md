# M09 — The repurposing engine

> **Module 9 of 20** · Part 2: Content Workflows
> **Estimated time:** 30 minutes
> **Outcome:** A one-page Repurposing Plan converting one idea into six outputs.

## Premise

One real idea can fuel a tweet, a short, a long-form video, a newsletter, and a sales-call asset — *if you set up the workflow once.* This module wires the previous three together. Most people miss this leverage.

## Concept — The repurposing graph

Each idea is a node. Outputs are edges. A single idea produces:

- **1 long-form video / blog post** — *the canonical artifact.*
- **3–5 shorts** — each lifting a single point from the long-form.
- **3–5 tweets** — the lessons in pure-text form.
- **1 newsletter section** — long-form's lesson + a personal aside.
- **1 sales asset** — the same lesson reframed as a customer ROI claim.

That's **one idea → six outputs.** Six chances for the idea to land. The math compounds across a year.

## Walkthrough — One idea, six outputs

**Idea:** "Why your dashboard's loading state is costing you trials."

| # | Output | How |
|---|--------|-----|
| 1 | **Long-form video** | Run M7's prompt with your YouTube transcripts. 12-minute essay. |
| 2 | **Shorts (×3)** | Pull the 3 cleanest soundbites from the long-form transcript. Run M8's prompt once per soundbite. |
| 3 | **Tweets (×5)** | Feed the long-form transcript into M6's tweet prompt. Ask for 5 tweets that each capture one lesson. |
| 4 | **Newsletter** | Take the long-form transcript + the top tweet. Adjust the tweet prompt's role to "newsletter writer in [your voice]." Generate. |
| 5 | **Sales asset** | Rewrite the central claim as a customer-facing ROI one-pager. (M20 covers this format.) |

## Exercise — The 1-to-6 challenge

1. Pick the next long-form you intend to produce. (Don't have one queued? Pick a podcast / interview transcript.)
2. **Before recording**, sketch the six derivatives in a one-page Repurposing Plan: which point becomes which short, which lessons become which tweets, etc.
3. Execute the plan. Schedule each output on its native platform on its native cadence.
4. Track which output drives the most response — that's a signal of which audience your idea actually resonates with.

**Acceptance test:** you can name the canonical long-form and point at the six derivatives that came from it.

> ✅ **Win pattern.** Doing M9 *before* M7 (planning the derivatives before recording) makes the long-form better. You record knowing it has to feed five children — you naturally include more complete examples and clearer takeaways.

## Knowledge check

1. Why is the long-form usually the canonical artifact, not the tweet?
2. Why does planning derivatives *before* the long-form improve the long-form?
3. What do you measure to learn which audience your idea resonates with?

### Answer key

1. Information density. A long-form has enough content to feed every smaller format. Tweets and shorts can't be expanded into long-form without padding.
2. Knowing you'll lift 3 shorts and 5 tweets out of the script forces you to include 3 self-contained soundbites and 5 self-contained lessons. Better structure flows from the constraint.
3. Per-output engagement (or replies, or referral traffic). The same idea on different platforms reveals different audiences. The platform with the strongest signal is the one to lean into.

## Takeaway

Stop creating "content." Create ideas. Then convert each idea into six outputs through this engine. The cost of the sixth output, after the first, is near zero.
