# M20 — Pricing, marketing, launch

> **Module 20 of 20** · Part 5: Production Readiness
> **Estimated time:** 90 minutes (plus the 21-day launch itself)
> **Outcome:** Defensible pricing tiers, launch assets ready to ship, and a 21-day launch calendar on your calendar.

## Premise

A SaaS that nobody pays for is a hobby. Pricing and packaging are not a final-day decision — they're a Day 1 decision that you'll revise on Day 30, Day 90, and forever.

## Concept — Pricing in three layers

| Layer | What you decide | Heuristic |
|-------|----------------|-----------|
| **Anchor** | The headline number on your pricing page. | 10x your cheapest plan from the highest plan. Sets perceived value. |
| **Plans** | 2–3 named tiers, each adding capacity or features. | Starter / Growth / Scale. Names matter; "Free" matters most. |
| **Mechanic** | Flat, per-seat, usage-based, hybrid. | Hybrid (small flat + metered) wins for most AI-powered SaaS in 2026. |

## Concept — Launch in five concrete moves

1. **Pre-launch list.** 50–300 emails of people who told you they want it. Use Resend + a one-page Next.js form.
2. **"Slow private."** Onboard 5–10 by hand. Watch them. Fix everything you see.
3. **Public soft launch.** Share to your audience: existing socials, newsletter, communities you're actually in.
4. **Coordinated channel launch.** Product Hunt, Hacker News, IndieHackers, your trade subreddit.
5. **After-action report.** What converted, what didn't, who churned in week 1, why. Write it down.

## Exercise — Your launch checklist

1. Set a launch date **21 days from today.**
2. Reverse-engineer the five moves above into a 21-day calendar.
3. Block your calendar accordingly. Treat launch week like surgery — nothing else on the schedule.
4. Inventory your launch assets. For each one missing, identify which prior module's workflow produces it.

**Acceptance test:** every launch asset has a row on your spreadsheet with the workflow that produced it and a "ready / draft / not started" status.

## Knowledge check

1. What's the rule of thumb for the anchor (highest tier) vs cheapest plan ratio?
2. Why is "slow private" with 5–10 hand-onboarded users a step *before* the public launch?
3. Why should every prior module's exercise feed into your launch?

### Answer key

1. ~10x. The anchor tier sets the ceiling of perceived value. A $79 / $249 / $999 spread (Starter at the cheap end, Cohort at $999) follows the rule.
2. Because the cost of finding a bug at scale is enormous and the cost of finding it in front of 5 people is zero. Fix everything those 5 people stumble on before the public sees it.
3. The launch IS the integration test. If your tweet workflow can't produce a launch thread, the workflow doesn't actually work yet — better to discover that before you depend on it.

## Takeaway

Launch is not a moment; it's a 21-day operation. Most products die from no-launch, not bad product. Set the date. Tell people. Show up.
